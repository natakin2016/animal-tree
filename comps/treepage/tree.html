The Parazoa-Eumetazoa split happened 940 million years ago.

Parazoa are similar to true animals as they too have differentiated cells. However, they lack organs and tissue. There is only one species of parazoa still living today.

The hallmark of the Eumetazoa is having true tissues, neurons, and a unique embryonic progression [gastrula stage]

Chordata is a large and diverse phylum (over 43,400 species)! Chances are, if you were asked to name an animal, it would likely be a member of this group. Chordates all exhibit a rudimentary backbone and nerve cord at some point during their development, seeing the structure mature into what we recognize as a “backbone” or spine.

Echinodermata include species of starfish, sand dollar, and urchins. They possess a remarkable ability to regenerate their organs and limbs.

Mollusca includes ocean creatures such as octopus, cuttlefish, and squid, as well as terrestrial species such as snails and slugs. Most species possess eyes, some lay eggs.
